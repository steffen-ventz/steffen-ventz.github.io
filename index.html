About me

I am an Assistant Professor of Biostatistisc in the Division of Biostatistics at the University of Minnesota.

Before joining UMN, I was a Faculty Member of the Department of Data Science at the Dana Farber Cancer Institute and the Department of Biostatistics at the Harvard T.H. School of Public Health.

Prior to HSPH and DFCI, I spent a couple of years as an Assistant Professor at the University of Rhode Island, where I was also affiliated with the Department of Computer Science and Statistics.

I obtained my PhD in Statistics from Bocconi University in Milan, Italy, under the supervision of Pietro Muliere and completed PostDoctoral trainning at the Harvard T.H. School of Public Health.

My research interests are mainly in response-adaptive clinical trials, statistical computation, Bayesian decision theory, Bayesian analyses and lately reinfored learning and causal inference.
